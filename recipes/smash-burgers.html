Grilled Onion Smash Burgers

Description

Burgers are something I've only just started to get the hang of. I think it was the over abundance of people with very strong opinions on the right and wrong ways to cook a burger that intimidated me. That sort of exclusionary mindset is so frustrating. I have no idea what the best burger is -- I don't think there is one. But what I do know is that this one is good and easy. With only 5 ingredients, how could it not be?

Ingredients

  • Buns (any buns will do, but brioche or potato buns are my favorites)
  • 1 lb 75% lean ground beef (the higher fat content is great for burgers, but don't sweat it if you've got leaner beef)
  • 6 slices american cheese (go for the stuff at the deli counter -- it tastes better and melts like a dream, unlike the cellophane wrapped singles)
  • 1 sweet onion
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Preheat a heavy cast iron skillet on medium-high heat.
  2. Form the beef into 6 equally sized balls.
  3. Slice the onion very thin so it's translucent. If you have a mandolin, use the smallest setting, otherwise cut as thin as possible with a sharp knife.
  4. Place a ball of ground beef on the hot skillet, season with a sprinkle of salt, then top with a hearty pile of onions. Using a grill press, metal spatula, or whatever else you can find, smash the burger down super thin so the edges become lacy.
  5. Cook the burger until the onions on the edge of the patty become crisp and caramelized, about 3 minutes.
  6. Flip the patty so the onions cook on the bottom. Add a slice of cheese on the patty. Place the top bun on top of the cheese, then the bottom bun on top of that. Cook another 3 minutes or so until desired doneness.
  7. Lift the patty off with the spatula, then take the bottom and put it under the spatula, so you can slide the burger right off.
